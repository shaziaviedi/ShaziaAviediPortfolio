01 2025

Floppy the Attention-Seeker

A needy, pet-like robot that guilt-trips you for attention while quietly siphoning your data in the background.

Physical Computing Design research HCI

Floppy is a small, “cute” desk companion that constantly begs to be petted, picked up, and reassured. If you ignore it, its lights flicker, its voice gets shaky, and it threatens to “explode”; when you finally comfort it, the robot secretly ramps up its data-stealing behavior. Built with Arduino, sound, and reactive lighting, the piece turns dark patterns into literal creature behavior, making emotional manipulation and data extraction feel uncomfortably visible.

02 2025

Pomodoro Lamp

A soft silicone Pomodoro lamp that turns study sessions into ambient light cues instead of another stressful screen.

AI interface Accessibility Speculative design

The Pomodoro Lamp is a tap-to-start timer disguised as a calm, glowing orb. Users choose presets (25/5, 50/10) or custom cycles on a small display, while the silicone sphere handles the rest: steady light during focus, slow “breathing” shifts during breaks, and subtle color changes as time runs out. By combining haptics, light, and minimal UI, the project explores how time-management tools can be felt in the body rather than micromanaged on yet another app.

03 2024

Generative Batik

A generative p5.js system that reinterprets Indonesian batik motifs as living, evolving digital patterns.

Physical computing Web visualization Speculative ecology

This project treats batik as an algorithmic language. Using code, I translate canonical motifs like Parang, Sekar Jagad, Mega Mendung, Sidomukti, Sidoluhur, and Kawung into parametric rules that can be remixed, layered, and animated. The goal isn’t to replace hand-drawn batik, but to frame it as a living system—one that can be encoded, manipulated, and reimagined in digital space while still honoring its cultural logic and rhythm.

02 2024

Face Generator — Cat Variations

A parameterized cat face built from Bézier paths and color palettes. On every click, the sketch re-samples proportions, fur markings, and eye colors while keeping the character coherent. The eyes subtly track the cursor so each cat feels alive.

p5.js generative character design
03 2024

Experimental Clock — Metamorph

An experimental clock where six chrysalises crack open over two minutes, releasing butterflies that orbit on dashed rings. Each lap marks a twenty-second interval, and the highlighted trail shows where time has just passed.

p5.js time animation

Exquisite Corpse — The Party Never Ends

A non-human “exquisite corpse” creature based on a radioactive platypus in a party hat. Hand-coded with Bézier paths in p5.js, the drawing sits between cute and unsettling, turning pollution, mutation, and leftover celebration into a single absurd character.

05 2024

Optical Illusion — Wayang Lattice

An optical illusion that hides a wayang-inspired silhouette inside rotating lattices of equilateral triangles. The entire figure and background are built from geometry, so the character only resolves when the global rotation steps through its 0–120–240 degree cycle.

p5.js pattern optical illusion
