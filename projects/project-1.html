Lost and Found — Gym from Memory

A compact gym reconstructed from my partner Edgar’s description only, using p5.js primitives, custom paths, and repeated treadmills.

Overview

Edgar described a classroom-sized rectangular gym: a short entrance, three treadmills in the front-left corner, a bike beside them, a mirror on the right wall, a bench press in front of it, and a rack with “around 20” dumbbells. I translated that language into a tidy top-view layout where cardio equipment lives on the left and strength equipment clusters near the mirror.

How it’s built

The room uses triangles and quads for walls and floor, plus a limited palette for floor, wall, equipment, and shadow. One treadmill is drawn in drawTreadmillShape() and reused three times with drawTreadmillAt(). The bike, bench press, and dumbbell rack are custom beginShape() paths, and a soft blendMode(ADD) highlight on the mirror hints at reflection.

Reflection

The prompt asked for a strict “top view,” but I nudged the drawing toward an isometric feel so machines read more clearly. I stylized spacing and dumbbell count to keep the composition clean, and most debugging came from layering shadows under equipment and turning one-off code into reusable functions so the scene stays editable.
