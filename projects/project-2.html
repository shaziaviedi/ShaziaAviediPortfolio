Face Generator — Cat Variations

A parameterized cat face that redraws itself on every click. Each version samples between a “minimum” and “maximum” drawing, while the eyes softly track the cursor.

Overview

This sketch starts from two reference drawings on a 400×400 grid: a “minimum” and “maximum” cat face. Every control point in the head, ears, fur patches, eyes, and whiskers has a lower and upper bound. On each mouse click, the program samples between those bounds to generate a new but still recognizably feline variation.

The face is built from closed Bézier paths for the head, ears, and markings; open Bézier paths for the jaw and whiskers; and ellipses for irises and pupils. A thick white frame and a pink background hold the character together visually.

How it works

The generator stores all geometry in a single parameter object P. Helpers like makePath() and makeSeg() interpolate between “min” and “max” anchor points and handles to produce each new path. After drawing the head, the code uses a generic clipToPath() function so ears, fur patterns, and eyes are all clipped to the correct regions without custom cases.

The eyes add a second layer of interaction: irises and pupils drift slightly toward the mouse. Because they’re rendered while clipped to each eyelid shape, they never spill out of the face, even when the cursor is at the edge of the canvas.

Reflection

Most of the work here was about designing a space of drawings rather than a single cat. Small constraints made a huge difference: enforcing “outward” cheeks relative to the head’s center kept silhouettes believable; anchoring the mouth to the nose avoided gaps; and capping pupil size as a fraction of iris size stopped proportions from drifting into the uncanny.

Cleanup utilities also ended up being crucial. A “tidy whisker” function aligns whisker roots near the nose, enforces a minimum reach, bows the handles outward, and limits vertical wobble so the lines feel deliberate but still varied. Overall, the project shifted my mindset from simply drawing a face to authoring a generative system with its own rules, failures, and personality.
